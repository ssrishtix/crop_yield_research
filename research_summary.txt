======================================================================
RESEARCH SUMMARY — Crop Yield Prediction & Price Forecasting
======================================================================

1. PRICE FORECASTING (ARIMA)
   The ARIMA model forecasts Erode turmeric prices 30 days ahead with
   a Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) of 5.20% and a
   Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of 632.02 Rs/quintal. This level
   of accuracy is suitable for short-term market guidance.

2. YIELD PREDICTION (Random Forest)
   The Random Forest model predicts crop yield with a MAE of
   126.79 kg/ha and MAPE of 1.56%. This enables
   farmers to estimate harvest volumes weeks in advance.

3. FEATURE IMPORTANCE (Ablation Study)
   The ablation study reveals the relative contribution of price-based
   and weather-based features. Removing either group increases RMSE,
   confirming that both feature classes carry complementary predictive
   information for yield estimation.

4. SEASONAL SELLING STRATEGY
   For Erode turmeric, the most profitable selling month is
   Apr, while the worst month is Jul.
   An informed farmer who times sales to the best month gains
   approximately 1,493 Rs/quintal (22.2%)
   compared to selling in the worst month.

5. PRICE SPREAD ANALYSIS
   Over the 12-year AGMARKNET dataset, the mean annual price spread
   (30-day minus 7-day rolling average) for turmeric is
   -25.51 Rs/quintal, indicating the degree of
   short-term vs medium-term price divergence.

======================================================================
KEY TAKEAWAY: Combining weather-aware yield prediction with seasonal
price analysis empowers smallholder farmers to optimise both
production planning and market timing, potentially increasing net
income by double-digit percentages.
